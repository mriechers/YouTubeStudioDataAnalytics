# Field Notes on Climate: Shortform Strategy Addendum

**Addendum to PBS Wisconsin Education Shortform Content Strategy Report**
**Prepared March 2026 | Launch Window: Now through Earth Day (April 22, 2026)**

---

## Why This Matters Now

Field Notes on Climate is launching in April 2026 with a built-in promotional moment (Earth Fest, April 20) and a culturally resonant anchor date (Earth Day, April 22). The production team has acknowledged that Shorts have been an afterthought so far. This addendum provides a concrete, week-by-week Shorts plan that integrates with the existing production timeline rather than adding to it.

The opportunity is significant: **this is the first Education series where Shorts can be designed into the launch from the start**, and the climate content niche has proven demand on this channel. Climate Wisconsin's "Adaptation and Mitigation" has 75,544 views — the #1 video on the entire Education channel — with zero companion Shorts. The FNoC teaser Short already posted in October has 1,611 views with 2.3% engagement, outperforming the Education channel Shorts average.

**The core argument**: A Shorts campaign around FNoC's launch doesn't just promote the series — it builds the Education channel's climate content brand and connects the new series to the proven demand that Climate Wisconsin's catalog has already demonstrated.

## Production Integration: Making Shorts Not Feel Like Extra Work

The producers have flagged Shorts as an afterthought. This is the most important section of this addendum — because a Shorts strategy that the production team can't or won't execute is no strategy at all.

### The "Moment Flagging" Workflow

**During production** (not after):
1. One team member (could be Mia in her Education Engagement Specialist role) watches for "Short-able moments" during filming or rough cut review
2. Flag the timecode and a one-line description: `14:23 — "Wait, that's how much water one pair of jeans takes?" [surprised reaction]`
3. At the end of each episode's production, there should be 3-5 flagged moments. The editor selects the strongest 2.

This takes 10 minutes during a process that's already happening. It does not require a separate production workflow.

### The Minimum Viable Short

Not every Short needs custom graphics, motion design, or re-editing. The minimum viable FNoC Short is:

1. A 35-50 second clip from episode footage
2. Burned-in captions (can be auto-generated and spot-checked)
3. A question-hook title with hashtags and pipe branding
4. A pinned comment linking to the full episode

**Estimated production time per Short**: 15-30 minutes (select clip, add captions, write title, publish, pin comment). This is a fraction of the time spent on full episode production.

### What NOT to Produce as Shorts

Based on the Education channel's performance data, avoid:
- **Preview/promo clips** that assume the viewer knows the series (avg 12 views on this channel)
- **Institutional announcements** ("About Field Notes on Climate") — these die on arrival
- **Sub-30-second teasers** that don't deliver a complete thought (avg 138 views)
- **Same-day batch uploads** — the Whoopensocker lesson: stagger by 2-3 days minimum

## Title and Metadata Standards

Every FNoC Short should follow this formula, based on Education channel performance data:

### Title Format
```
[Question or curiosity hook] #hashtag1 #hashtag2 | Field Notes on Climate
```

**Examples**:
- "Is your Wisconsin well water safe to drink? #waterquality #PFAS | Field Notes on Climate"
- "How many gallons of water does one pair of jeans take? #fastfashion #climatechange | Field Notes on Climate"
- "Why are Wisconsin's pollinators disappearing? #pollinators #science | Field Notes on Climate"

### Hashtag Strategy

**Primary hashtags** (use 1-2 per Short, topic-specific):
- `#climatechange`, `#science`, `#environment`
- Topic-specific: `#PFAS`, `#waterquality`, `#fastfashion`, `#pollinators`, `#farming`, `#wildrice`, `#soilhealth`

**Secondary hashtags** (use 0-1 per Short, audience-targeting):
- `#Wisconsin`, `#education`, `#sustainability`

**Do not over-hashtag.** Research shows 2-3 targeted hashtags outperform 5+ generic ones.

### Description Template
```
[One sentence expanding on the hook]

Watch the full episode: [link]
Classroom resources: [PBSLM link]

Field Notes on Climate explores impacts of our changing climate and shares solutions-oriented stories from across Wisconsin. Made with and for educators.
```

### Pinned Comment Template
```
Want to go deeper? Watch the full episode: [link]
Teachers: Find classroom resources at [PBSLM collection link]
```

### Critical Data Gap

The local analytics database does not contain **completion rate**, **swiped-away ratio**, or **subscriber source** data — the three metrics most important for Shorts optimization. These are available in YouTube Studio's native analytics. For FNoC specifically, we recommend:

1. **Check YouTube Studio weekly** during launch for completion rate and swiped-away data on each FNoC Short
2. **Track which Shorts drive full-episode views** via YouTube Studio's "How viewers find your videos" → Traffic sources
3. **Log these metrics manually** until they can be pulled into the database via the Analytics API

## Coordination Notes

### With Existing Climate Wisconsin Content
The Climate Wisconsin catalog Shorts (Phase 1, Week 1) serve double duty: they create companion Shorts for the channel's highest-performing content AND they prime the algorithm for FNoC. The description on these Shorts should include a forward reference: *"A new climate series is coming this April — Field Notes on Climate."*

### With The Ways
The Wild Rice episode creates a natural bridge to The Ways, the channel's #1 series by average views. When the Wild Rice Short is published, a Community post on the Education channel linking to both FNoC and The Ways content can cross-pollinate audiences.

### With the Main PBS Wisconsin Channel
The PFAS/water quality coverage on the main channel (13,037 views on the Here & Now segment, 5,686 views on the companion Short) demonstrates proven audience demand for Wisconsin water quality content. When the FNoC Water Quality episode launches, consider whether a cross-channel Community post on the main PBS Wisconsin channel can drive the main channel's audience to the Education channel.

### With PBSLM
Every FNoC Short should link to the PBSLM collection (launching Earth Day) in its pinned comment. The Shorts serve as discovery mechanisms for classroom resources — this is a unique value proposition that distinguishes PBS Wisconsin's Shorts from entertainment-focused content.

### With the Livestream Production
Assign one team member during each premiere window to flag "Short-able moments" in real time. The goal is to have 2 same-day Shorts published from premiere footage. These don't need to be polished — the live energy is the value.
