Executive Summary

In 2025, Wisconsin Life shifted from a clustered publishing model (tied to broadcast seasons) to a weekly content schedule. The results are clear: fewer videos, significantly better performance.

The weekly model has doubled audience engagement and nearly quintupled content velocity, while maintaining a higher per-video average despite producing 85% fewer videos.

Part 4: Big Wins

1. Food/Drink Content: From Worst to First

Average views went from 425 (2023) to 3,832 (2025) — a 9x improvement. The Navy cook's veterans pub (26K), Windmill Ice Cream (1.8K), Sun Drop bottling family (855), and Bigfoot believers (5K, food-adjacent) are landing in ways 2023 food content never did. The winning formula: food + nostalgia + a specific Wisconsin place.

2. Competitions/Spectacle Found Their Groove

Battle of the Buses (5.9K), Guinness World Record motorcyclist (2.9K), Watercross (2.1K). In 2023, the sports/competition theme averaged 979 views; in 2025 it's 3,616. These stories have a built-in narrative arc and visual spectacle that drives clicks.

The audience isn't just watching — they're responding. More engagement drives more algorithmic recommendations, which drives more views.

4. January Proved What a Concentrated Push Can Do

11 videos in January averaged 4,146 views — nearly 5x the rest-of-year average (869). The Navy cook hit 26K and likely pulled subscribers back to the channel, lifting adjacent uploads. This suggests a hybrid approach: maintain the weekly cadence but do an intentional "season premiere" push with your strongest content in January.

5. Nature/Outdoors Became a Real Category

From 12 videos at 403 avg views (2023) to 8 videos at 1,120 (2025). Old growth forest walks, loon research, Ice Age geology, dark sky reserves. The difference is specificity and stakes ("loons are disappearing," "fight light pollution").

Part 5: Missed Opportunities

1. The 56-Day Spring Gap (April 7 – June 3)

The weekly cadence completely broke down for nearly two months. This was the biggest gap of 2025, erasing momentum from a strong January-March run. Other notable gaps: 18 days in late January, 13 days in March. Cadence discipline slips consistently.

2. History/Heritage Content Collapsed

Recommendations

Quick Wins (no production changes needed)

  1. Shift Thursday publishes to Tuesday — consistent signal across 3 years of data; 24% of 2025 videos published on the worst-performing day
  2. Tighten to 3.5-5 minute target — this duration range gets 2.2x the views of 5-7 minute pieces in 2025
  3. Fix Shorts data classification — update the database to use YouTube's actual content_type API field for accurate metrics

Content Strategy

  1. Revive history/heritage content — 3 videos in 2025 vs. 13 in 2023; proven demand exists
  2. Double down on food + place + nostalgia — 9x improvement confirms this is the winning formula
  3. Increase wildlife/animal volume — only 6 pieces in 2025; every one that was published performed
  4. Maintain competition/spectacle pieces but pair with deeper human stories — spectacle drives reach; human connection drives loyalty

Scheduling

  1. Close the spring gap — the 56-day April-June hole broke momentum; plan content bridge for this period
  2. Keep the January premiere push — it amplified rather than cannibalized; lead with strongest content
  3. Target 48+ active weeks — 2025 hit 40; the remaining gaps represent lost audience habit-building

Shorts Strategy

  1. Stop making companion/promo Shorts — they consistently underperform their longform counterparts
  2. Every Short should tell a complete micro-story — the Miniature Horse formula (animals + charm + visual payoff + exclamatory title) is proven
  3. Establish a consistent Shorts cadence — 2-3 per week, published early in the week
  4. Title formula for Shorts — short (<40 chars), action verb, exclamation point, no hashtags
