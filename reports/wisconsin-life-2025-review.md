# Wisconsin Life: 2025 Year in Review

**Prepared for the Wisconsin Life Landing Meeting**
**Data through March 5, 2026 | Source: PBS Wisconsin YouTube Analytics Database**

---

## Executive Summary

In 2025, Wisconsin Life shifted from a clustered publishing model (tied to broadcast seasons) to a weekly content schedule. The results are clear: **fewer videos, significantly better performance.**

| Metric | 2023 (clustered) | 2025 (weekly) | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Videos published | 433 | 66 | -85% |
| Avg views/video | 1,265 | 1,415 | **+12%** |
| Avg like rate | 1.75% | 3.58% | **+105%** |
| Views per day (velocity) | 1.19 | 5.72 | **+381%** |
| % videos with comments | 38% | 73% | **+92%** |
| Active weeks (of 52) | 23 | 40 | **+74%** |

The weekly model has doubled audience engagement and nearly quintupled content velocity, while maintaining a higher per-video average despite producing 85% fewer videos.

## Part 4: Big Wins

### 1. Food/Drink Content: From Worst to First

Average views went from 425 (2023) to 3,832 (2025) — a **9x improvement**. The Navy cook's veterans pub (26K), Windmill Ice Cream (1.8K), Sun Drop bottling family (855), and Bigfoot believers (5K, food-adjacent) are landing in ways 2023 food content never did. The winning formula: **food + nostalgia + a specific Wisconsin place**.

### 2. Competitions/Spectacle Found Their Groove

Battle of the Buses (5.9K), Guinness World Record motorcyclist (2.9K), Watercross (2.1K). In 2023, the sports/competition theme averaged 979 views; in 2025 it's 3,616. These stories have a built-in narrative arc and visual spectacle that drives clicks.

### 3. The Engagement Transformation

| Metric | 2023 | 2025 | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Like rate | 1.75% | 3.58% | +105% |
| % videos with comments | 38% | 73% | +92% |
| Views per day | 1.19 | 5.72 | +381% |

The audience isn't just watching — they're responding. More engagement drives more algorithmic recommendations, which drives more views.

### 4. January Proved What a Concentrated Push Can Do

11 videos in January averaged 4,146 views — nearly 5x the rest-of-year average (869). The Navy cook hit 26K and likely pulled subscribers back to the channel, lifting adjacent uploads. This suggests a hybrid approach: maintain the weekly cadence but do an intentional "season premiere" push with your strongest content in January.

### 5. Nature/Outdoors Became a Real Category

From 12 videos at 403 avg views (2023) to 8 videos at 1,120 (2025). Old growth forest walks, loon research, Ice Age geology, dark sky reserves. The difference is specificity and stakes ("loons are *disappearing*," "fight light pollution").

## Part 5: Missed Opportunities

### 1. The 56-Day Spring Gap (April 7 – June 3)

The weekly cadence completely broke down for nearly two months. This was the biggest gap of 2025, erasing momentum from a strong January-March run. Other notable gaps: 18 days in late January, 13 days in March. Cadence discipline slips consistently.

### 2. History/Heritage Content Collapsed

| Year | Videos | Avg Views |
|---|---|---|
| 2023 | 13 | 1,880 |
| 2025 | 3 | 433 |

This was a top-3 theme in 2023 (Hmong Culture 11.1K, German-Oshkosh 18.6K). In 2025, only 3 heritage pieces were published and they averaged just 433 views. Meanwhile, the channel-wide "The Look Back" history Shorts maintain an 81% hit rate. History content has a proven audience — Wisconsin Life stopped serving it.

### 3. Animals/Wildlife Volume Dropped

| Year | Videos | Avg Views |
|---|---|---|
| 2023 | 32 | 2,831 |
| 2025 | 6 | 927 |

The 2023 Muskie Legend (60.9K) was the biggest Wisconsin Life video ever. Only 6 wildlife videos in all of 2025 — the loons piece (2.4K) and alpaca farm (1.1K) both worked; there just weren't enough of them.

### 4. High Reach, Low Engagement Videos

Three 2025 videos got 2,000+ views but sub-1.5% like rates:

| Title | Views | Like % | Comments |
|---|---|---|---|
| Battle of the Buses | 5,852 | 0.94% | 1 |
| Bigfoot believers | 4,957 | 1.47% | 8 |
| Watercross | 2,114 | 0.80% | 1 |

These "spectacle" videos drive reach but don't build audience loyalty. Compare with Ojibwe ponies (6.3K views, 5.26% likes, 24 comments). Spectacle pieces need to be balanced with high-engagement human stories.

### 5. Duration Mismatch

The sweet spot flipped from 5-7 minutes (2023) to 3.5-5 minutes (2025), but 35% of 2025 content is still in the 5-7 minute range where it now underperforms (848 avg views vs. 1,858 for 3.5-5 min).

### 6. High-Engagement Stories That Never Found Their Audience

## Recommendations

### Quick Wins (no production changes needed)

1. **Shift Thursday publishes to Tuesday** — consistent signal across 3 years of data; 24% of 2025 videos published on the worst-performing day
2. **Tighten to 3.5-5 minute target** — this duration range gets 2.2x the views of 5-7 minute pieces in 2025
3. **Fix Shorts data classification** — update the database to use YouTube's actual `content_type` API field for accurate metrics

### Content Strategy

4. **Revive history/heritage content** — 3 videos in 2025 vs. 13 in 2023; proven demand exists
5. **Double down on food + place + nostalgia** — 9x improvement confirms this is the winning formula
6. **Increase wildlife/animal volume** — only 6 pieces in 2025; every one that was published performed
7. **Maintain competition/spectacle pieces but pair with deeper human stories** — spectacle drives reach; human connection drives loyalty

### Scheduling

8. **Close the spring gap** — the 56-day April-June hole broke momentum; plan content bridge for this period
9. **Keep the January premiere push** — it amplified rather than cannibalized; lead with strongest content
10. **Target 48+ active weeks** — 2025 hit 40; the remaining gaps represent lost audience habit-building

### Shorts Strategy

11. **Stop making companion/promo Shorts** — they consistently underperform their longform counterparts
12. **Every Short should tell a complete micro-story** — the Miniature Horse formula (animals + charm + visual payoff + exclamatory title) is proven
13. **Establish a consistent Shorts cadence** — 2-3 per week, published early in the week
14. **Title formula for Shorts** — short (<40 chars), action verb, exclamation point, no hashtags
