## Executive Summary

This report synthesizes three lines of analysis into a single comprehensive reference for the PBS Wisconsin Education channel: a COPPA/Made for Kids compliance audit, a performance diagnostic identifying what drives hits and what suppresses them, and program-by-program recommendations covering all major series.

The Education channel has 253 videos across roughly 20 named series. Its highest-performing content by velocity is a cluster of short-form Wisconsin history clips from The Look Back — a recent format pivot that is definitively working. Its most valuable legacy content — The Ways, Climate Wisconsin, aka Teacher — continues to draw steady search traffic from educators and students years after publication, demonstrating durable curriculum demand.

Those two strengths are being undermined by a single structural problem: 102 of 253 videos (40%) carry a Made for Kids flag, including most of the channel's top-performing series. MFK disables comments, end screens, cards, personalized recommendations, and notification bells. Three of the channel's four most-viewed series are affected. Correcting misclassified MFK flags requires no production budget and no new content — it is the single highest-leverage action available.

**Four findings that drive most of the recommendations:**

1. **40% MFK rate is the top suppressor.** An estimated 60–80 of the 102 MFK-flagged videos are likely misclassified. The affected series (The Ways, Climate Wisconsin, Wisconsin Biographies, Meet the Lab, Wisconsin Hometown Stories Youth Media) serve educators and middle/high school students — not children under 13 as a primary audience. Correction restores engagement features to the channel's strongest performers.

2. **The Look Back's short-form pivot is the channel's most successful recent strategy.** Short clips (under 90 seconds) from this series post velocity numbers between 21 and 265 views/day — far exceeding anything else on the channel. The companion long-form episodes (8–10 minutes, all MFK-flagged) average under 1 view/day. The format split within a single series is a clear signal about what the audience wants.

3. **Three legacy series are proven search anchors.** The Ways, Climate Wisconsin, and aka Teacher continue drawing meaningful daily traffic years after upload. This is textbook evergreen curriculum content. They deserve optimization investment (chapters, updated descriptions, SEO refresh) rather than being left as-is while MFK suppresses their growth.

4. **Webinar recordings and preview clips are format mismatches.** Raw uploads of 46–72 minute webinars and standalone preview clips from longer episodes underperform universally. These formats do not belong on YouTube as uploaded.

## Section 1: Made for Kids Audit

### Overview

| Category | Count | Notes |
|----------|-------|-------|
| Total Education channel videos | 253 | |

| Correctly classified MFK=True | ~20–40 | Kindness Curriculum, Whoopensocker, some Uncategorized |
| Likely misclassified MFK=True | ~60–80 | See series detail below |

A 40% MFK rate is unusually high for a channel whose primary audience is K–12 educators and students above age 13. COPPA/YouTube's "made for kids" designation applies to content **directed to children under 13 as its primary audience** — not content used in schools, not content about children's topics, and not content produced by or featuring young people.

### COPPA Standards Applied (Sources: YouTube Help, vidIQ, AIR.io)

Per YouTube's guidance, the following factors determine whether content is directed to children: subject matter, visual content, music, use of animated characters or celebrities appealing to children, language/complexity level, and whether it is marketed or described as for children. Educational content used in K–12 classrooms is not automatically "made for kids" — the platform distinction is about **primary audience**, not deployment context.

## Section 2: What Makes a Hit — and What Holds Content Back

### Hit Patterns on the Education Channel

**1. Short-form Look Back clips (under 90 seconds)**
The channel's velocity leaders are almost entirely Look Back Shorts: specific, titled historical facts about Wisconsin presented in under 90 seconds with search-optimized hashtags (#history #science #Wisconsin). The top video hits 265.8 views/day. These are functioning as a discovery machine — viewers searching for Wisconsin history, American history, or Indigenous history land on these clips, watch them fully (high retention for short content), and potentially follow to the channel or longer content.

**2. Evergreen curriculum search anchors**
The Ways and Climate Wisconsin are 5–6 years old and still generating 7–34 views/day. This is textbook search-discovery behavior: teachers planning a unit on Indigenous culture or climate science search YouTube, find these videos, assign them, and the cycle repeats every school year. This content does not go stale. It compounds.

**3. Culturally significant content with strong search demand**
aka Teacher (Ableism, 21.5 views/day), the Ladson-Billings video (25.1 views/day), The Ways' Indigenous culture content — these address specific topics (ableism, culturally responsive pedagogy, Indigenous traditions) with dedicated educator search communities. Teachers searching "ableism video for class" or "culturally relevant teaching" will find this content and return to it seasonally.

**4. Compact format (under 6 minutes)**
Nearly every top-velocity video is either a Short (under 90 seconds) or a compact curriculum doc (3–6 minutes). Nothing in the 8–10 minute range is succeeding on this channel, including the Look Back companion episodes.

### What Holds Education Content Back

**1. MFK misclassification (the biggest suppressor)**
102 videos — including the channel's highest-performing series — have all engagement and recommendation features disabled. This is not a content problem. It is a settings error.

**2. Long-form companion episodes (8–10 minutes)**
The Look Back's companion episodes are the clearest case study: the Shorts from this series post 20–265 views/day; the companion episodes average under 1 view/day. Both cover the same Wisconsin history topics. The difference is format and length. The audience for 90-second Wisconsin history facts on YouTube is not looking for a 10-minute exploration of the same topic.

**3. Raw webinar uploads**
Educator Connection LIVE! averages 46 minutes. Building Background Knowledge averages 72 minutes. These are not edited for YouTube; they are full recordings of live events. Despite some decent total view counts (Educator Connection LIVE! has 20,791 total views across 7 videos), the format is a mismatch for YouTube discovery. Views likely come from direct links distributed to event attendees, not YouTube search.

**4. Preview clips as standalone uploads**
Eight "preview from The Look Back" clips average around 10 views each. Teaser content without a self-contained narrative serves no purpose as a standalone YouTube video. It splits search results and dilutes the channel's playlist structure.

**5. Meet the Lab's format problem**
12 videos, 92 average views, 0.07 views/day. Even without MFK suppression, this series has not found an audience. The format (short STEM exploration clips) is not differentiated enough to compete for search traffic against national science education channels.

### The Ways
**Current assessment:** Channel's #1 series by total views (203,130, 14 videos, 14,509 avg). Still generating 7–17+ views/day on 5-6 year old content. Top performers: "Prayers in a Song" (17.5/day), "Powwow Trail" (16.4/day), "Manoomin" (15.2/day). 12 of 14 videos are MFK=True.

**What works:** Evergreen demand from educators teaching about Indigenous cultures, Native American studies, and Wisconsin history. Content is high-quality, culturally specific, and not easily replicated by general-purpose educational channels. PBS's authoritative source status helps with ranking.

**What doesn't work:** MFK flags on 12 of 14 videos eliminate end screens, comments, and recommendations. Viewers who find "Manoomin" cannot be directed to "Prayers in a Song" via end screens. The series cannot build on its own momentum.

**Recommendations:**
1. Change MFK=False on all 12 flagged videos immediately (Priority 2 in the correction queue). This is a settings change, not a production task.
2. Add end screens to all 14 videos directing viewers to related episodes within the series. Build a dedicated The Ways playlist and add it to end screens.
3. Audit and refresh descriptions with curriculum keywords (unit topics, grade levels, subjects: "Indigenous Wisconsin," "Native American culture," "Wisconsin history," "K-12 curriculum") to strengthen SEO for teacher search.

**MFK recommendation:** MFK=False. Primary audience is educators and older students, not children under 13.

### Climate Wisconsin
**Current assessment:** 9 videos, 77,823 total views, 8,647 avg — but every single video is MFK=True. Top performer: "Adaptation and Mitigation" at 34.1 views/day, the channel's third-highest velocity video. The entire series is suppressed.

**What works:** Climate science is a high-demand curriculum topic with sustained teacher search interest. PBS's credibility on science content gives these videos authoritative source ranking advantages. The content is genuinely evergreen — climate science units are taught every year.

**What doesn't work:** 100% MFK flag rate on a series with the channel's best-performing video. "Adaptation and Mitigation" is getting 34.1 views/day with no end screens, no comments, and no recommendation eligibility. The series has a growth ceiling imposed by a classification error.

**Recommendations:**
1. Change MFK=False on all 9 videos (Priority 1 in the correction queue — highest-impact single action on the channel).
2. Add chapters to longer episodes. Add end screens across the series linking to related Climate Wisconsin episodes.
3. Consider companion Shorts (60–90 seconds) that excerpt key concepts from the full episodes — following The Look Back model. "What is the difference between climate adaptation and mitigation?" as a 60-second clip could drive traffic to the full episode.

**MFK recommendation:** MFK=False. Content features adult scientists, complex scientific topics, and is produced for middle school, high school, and college educators.

### aka Teacher
**Current assessment:** 10 videos, 54,841 total views, 5,484 avg, 3.71 views/day average. No MFK flags. Top performer: "Ableism" at 21.5 views/day. Strongest engagement metrics on the channel — teacher/educator audience actively searches for this content.

**What doesn't work:** Nothing structurally broken. The series is underoptimized relative to its demand — descriptions and chapters likely could be strengthened to capture more search traffic.

2. Add chapters to any videos over 6 minutes. Chapters generate Google snippet previews and improve retention.
3. Create a curated aka Teacher playlist and link it in all video descriptions and end screens. The series has 10 strong videos — viewers finding one should easily find the rest.

**MFK recommendation:** MFK=False (already correct). Maintain.

### The Look Back
**Current assessment:** 52 videos, 51,623 total views — but deeply bifurcated by format. Short clips (under 90 seconds) post 21–265 views/day. Long-form companion episodes (8–10 minutes, 11 videos, all MFK=True) average under 1 view/day. The series also has at least 8 preview clips averaging ~10 views each, which dilute the channel.

**What works:** The short-form format pivot is the channel's most successful recent strategy. The clips succeed because they are specific (a single historical fact or artifact), short enough for full completion, and optimized with hashtags for search discovery. The title format ("How Milwaukee shaped modern keyboard design") signals clearly what the viewer will learn.

**What doesn't work:** The long-form companion episodes are not performing. MFK flags worsen an already struggling format. The preview clip uploads add clutter without value.

**Recommendations:**
1. Change MFK=False on all 11 long-form companion episodes. Even if the format continues to struggle, removing the flag allows the algorithm to test distribution.
2. Audit and consider removing or unlisting the 8 standalone preview clips. They average ~10 views each and create a confusing viewer experience.
3. Continue the Short-form format for new Look Back content — the data is unambiguous. Prioritize the 60–90 second single-fact format over 8–10 minute companion episodes.
4. For the companion episodes: add chapters and revise descriptions to function as reference content for educators who have already watched the Shorts and want deeper context. Frame them explicitly as "full episode" companions in titles.

**MFK recommendation:** Change to MFK=False for all 11 long-form companions. The Shorts (already MFK=False) are correctly classified.

## Appendix: COPPA and MFK Reference Standards

**Definition of "made for kids" per YouTube/FTC COPPA standards:**
Content is directed to children when its subject matter, visual content, music, use of animated characters or child-focused celebrities, language complexity, and marketing are primarily oriented toward children under 13. The key word is *primary* audience.

Content used in K–12 education is not automatically "made for kids." The test is whether the content is **directed to** children as its primary audience, not whether it can be used with children.

**What MFK=True disables:**
- Comments
- End screens
- Cards
- Personalized recommendations ("Up next")
- Notification bell alerts to subscribers
- Interest-based advertising (replaced with contextual-only ads, reducing CPM)

**Practical implication for Education channel:**
Any series where the primary audience is educators, older students, or general adults — even if the content is used in classrooms — should be MFK=False. This applies to the overwhelming majority of the Education channel's content.
