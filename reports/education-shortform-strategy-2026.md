# PBS Wisconsin Education: Shortform Content Strategy Report

**Prepared March 2026 | Source: PBS Wisconsin YouTube Analytics Database + Shortform Content Research Corpus**

## Executive Summary

PBS Wisconsin Education's shortform strategy is a **one-show story with untapped potential across the catalog**. The Look Back drives nearly all successful Shorts, while dormant series (The Ways, Climate Wisconsin, aka Teacher) account for 63% of the channel's lifetime views but produce zero Shorts. The incoming Field Notes on Climate series offers a greenfield opportunity to launch with shortform built into the production workflow from day one.

### Data Quality Note

The `is_short` flag in the analytics database uses duration-based detection (<=60 seconds) rather than YouTube's native Shorts classification. This report uses duration filtering and distinguishes between true Shorts (<=60s), extended short-form (61-180s), and longform (>180s). Performance comparisons should be interpreted with this caveat.

## Part 1: Program-by-Program Analysis

### The Look Back — The Shorts Engine

**Status**: Active (Season 3, FY26) | **52 total videos** | **26 Shorts**

The Look Back is the only series currently producing Shorts at scale, and it's working. Shorts outperform the show's longform content by nearly 5x on reach.

| Format | Count | Avg Views | Avg Engagement Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Shorts (<=60s) | 26 | 1,642 | 1.74% |
| Longform (>3 min) | 26 | 344 | 2.06% |

#### What's Working

**The formula**: A surprising Wisconsin history fact, told in 35-57 seconds, with a curiosity hook in the title, relevant hashtags, and pipe-branded to the show.

**Top 5 Look Back Shorts:**

| Title | Views | Engagement | Duration | Views/Day |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Why does Wisconsin have a George Washington portrait? #art #history \| The Look Back | 5,687 | 3.75% | 46s | 38.1 |
| The Northwest Passage \| The Look Back | 2,769 | 1.70% | 56s | — |
| A British medal for a Menominee chief #AmericanRevolution #NativeHistory \| The Look Back | 2,768 | 1.23% | 54s | 29.7 |
| Going beyond 'no taxation without representation' #americanhistory \| The Look Back | 2,727 | 1.32% | 56s | — |
| What role did #Wisconsin play in the #AmericanRevolution? \| The Look Back | 1,973 | 1.47% | 49s | — |

**Emerging velocity winners** (published in the last 30 days):

| Title | Views/Day | Total Views |
|---|---|---|
| Wisconsin's oldest metalworking tradition | 252.7 | New |
| How glaciers revealed Wisconsin's ancient copper | 30.2 | New |
| How Milwaukee shaped modern keyboard design | 30.1 | 1,810 |

The science-history crossover content ("metalworking tradition," "glaciers + copper") is showing the strongest recent velocity, suggesting a content direction worth expanding.

#### What's Not Working

**Preview clips are dead weight.** 10 Look Back Season 1 preview clips from March 2024 average **12 views each**. These are promotional excerpts that assume the viewer already knows the show. The YouTube Shorts algorithm has nothing to recommend them for.

| Preview Clip | Views |
|---|---|
| "An Iron in the Fire" preview from "The Look Back" | 6 |
| "For the Record" preview from "The Look Back" | 6 |
| "Time to Shine" preview from "The Look Back" | 7 |
| "Waterlogged" preview from "The Look Back" | 8 |

#### Recommendations for The Look Back

1. **Increase Shorts cadence to 3-4 per week** — The show's micro-story format is purpose-built for Shorts. Current cadence of ~2/week leaves algorithmic momentum on the table.
2. **Lean into science-history crossovers** — The metalworking/copper/glacier cluster is showing the highest recent velocity. "How Wisconsin's glaciers created the Driftless Area," "Why Lake Mendota freezes the way it does" — this niche is underserved on YouTube.
3. **Systematize the "Bet you didn't know Wisconsin..." hook** — The best performers all connect something universally known to Wisconsin in a surprising way (QWERTY keyboards, George Washington, the Northwest Passage). This can be a repeatable content series.
4. **Always use question-based titles** — Only 2 of 26 Look Back Shorts use question titles, but they average **3,830 views** vs. 1,460 for statements (2.6x). The sample is small but directionally clear.
5. **Stop producing preview/promo Shorts entirely** — Promote full episodes via Community posts, cards, or endscreens instead.
6. **Package as a YouTube Course** — Structure episodes by era (American Revolution, Progressive Era, etc.) for classroom discovery. Requires no new production.

### Field Notes on Climate — The Greenfield Opportunity

**Status**: Active pre-production | **Launch: April 2026**

This is a new multimedia series exploring climate impacts and solutions-oriented stories from across Wisconsin, made with and for educators. The YouTube livestream soft launch is planned for April 2026, with the official website + PBSLM collection launching on Earth Day (April 22).

**This is the first Education series where Shorts can be designed into the production workflow from the start**, rather than retrofitted from existing footage.

#### Recommendations for Field Notes on Climate

1. **Build a Shorts-first production plan** — For every episode, identify 2-3 moments that can be captured as standalone 30-45 second Shorts during production. Don't retrofit later.
2. **Launch with a Shorts teaser campaign** — Before the April livestream, release 3-4 Shorts that introduce the series' themes. Use the question-hook formula: "What's happening to Wisconsin's winters?" "Why are Wisconsin farmers changing what they plant?"
3. **Burned-in captions from day one** — The research corpus shows 12-15% higher completion rates with captions. For CVAA compliance on broadcast-originated content, this is also a legal requirement.
4. **Coordinate with Climate Wisconsin catalog** — Release Climate Wisconsin Shorts in the same window to create a thematic cluster that signals "climate content" to the algorithm.
5. **Pin comments linking to the PBSLM collection** — Every Short should link educators to the full classroom resources.

### Whoopensocker — Needs a Reset

**Status**: Active in production pipeline | **11 videos** | **7 flagged as Shorts**

Whoopensocker launched all 11 videos on a single day (September 26, 2025) and has struggled to gain traction. The series averages just **83 views/video** with very low velocity (0.1-3.9 views/day).

| Issue | Detail |
|---|---|
| Same-day batch upload | All 11 videos uploaded September 26, 2025 — no algorithmic runway |
| Generic titles | "Sushi," "Pass the Clap," "Name and Movement" — zero discoverability |
| Duration mismatch | 72-180 seconds — too long for Shorts algorithm, too short for longform value |
| No hashtags or show branding | None of the titles use hashtags or the `| Whoopensocker` pipe format |
| PBSLM upload stalled | Awaiting approval since September 2025 (144+ days) |

#### Recommendations for Whoopensocker

1. **Re-title and re-tag existing videos** — Add descriptive, searchable titles: "Sushi" → "Sushi Classroom Game: Get Kids Moving! #PhysEd #ClassroomGames | Whoopensocker"
2. **Stagger future uploads** — Never batch-upload. Space videos 2-3 days apart to give each one algorithmic testing time.
3. **Cut true Shorts (<=45s) from existing clips** — Extract the single best 30-second moment from each activity and publish as a companion Short.
4. **Target the PE/recess teacher niche** — This is an underserved audience on YouTube. Hashtags: `#PhysEd`, `#BrainBreak`, `#ClassroomGames`, `#Recess`
5. **Unblock the PBSLM upload** — 144 days in approval limbo means zero classroom discoverability.

## Part 2: Cross-Program Strategy

### The Title Formula That Works

Data from all Education Shorts shows clear patterns:

| Title Element | Avg Views With | Avg Views Without | Lift |
|---|---|---|---|
| Question mark (?) | 3,830 | 1,460 | **+162%** |
| Hashtags (#) | 1,949 | 1,334 | **+46%** |
| Pipe branding (\|) | ~1,600 | ~200 | **~8x** |

**The winning formula**: `[Question or curiosity hook] #relevant #hashtags | [Show Name]`

### Publishing Cadence

| Day | Avg Views | Hit Rate |
|---|---|---|
| **Wednesday** | 1,941 | 50% of all hits |
| **Monday** | 1,492 | 21% of hits |
| Tuesday-Thursday | 700-1,200 | Moderate |
| **Saturday-Sunday** | 16-29 | 0% of hits |

**Recommendation**: Publish Shorts Monday-Wednesday only. Never on weekends.

### The Duration Sweet Spot

| Duration Bucket | Avg Views | Count |
|---|---|---|
| 0-15 seconds | Low sample | — |
| 16-30 seconds | 138 | Too short to deliver value |
| **31-45 seconds** | 800 | Good for simple concepts |
| **46-60 seconds** | 1,401 | Best overall performance |
| 61-90 seconds | Variable | Extended format, test carefully |
| 91-180 seconds | Low | Too long for Shorts algorithm |

**Target range**: 35-57 seconds for most content.

### Research-Backed Best Practices

From the shortform content research corpus (6 platform reports, ~290KB):

| Practice | Research Finding | PBS Wisconsin Education Application |
|---|---|---|
| Burned-in captions | +12-15% completion rate | Mandatory for broadcast-originated content (CVAA). Add to all Shorts. |
| 3-second hook | First 3 seconds determine "swipe away" signal | Strip institutional openings (title cards, music, logos). Lead with the surprising fact. |
| Pinned comment with link | Up to 74% higher subscriber conversion | Every Short should pin a comment linking to the full episode or PBSLM resource. |
| Completion rate target | 80%+ is "strong" for educational content | Shorter is better. A 40-second Short with 85% completion beats a 58-second Short with 60%. |
| Cross-posting ≠ strategy | Each platform has different norms and discovery mechanics | YouTube Shorts should be YouTube-native, not repurposed TikToks. |
| Shorts RPM | $0.01-$0.07 per 1,000 views | Shorts are audience builders, not revenue. Measure by reach and PBSLM traffic, not dollars. |
